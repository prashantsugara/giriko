2. Categories of Personal Data We Collect
To manifest your rituals in the void, we collect the following categories of personal information:

User Generated Content (UGC): Videos recorded via the ritual camera, captions you provide, and comments ("Whispers") you leave.

Technical & Usage Data: Device identifiers, crash logs, and app performance metrics.
3. Why We Process Your Personal Data (Purpose)
We collect and process your personal data for the following specific purposes:

To Provide Core Services: Enabling you to record rituals, post content, and interact with the "If Wishes Could Kill" social experience.
Localization: Using approximate location to separate users into localized ritual feeds (Sanctuaries).
Personalization: Saving your preferences, such as your chosen "Ritual Accents".
Advertising: To show relevant, personalized, or non-personalized advertisements via Google AdMob to keep the app free.
Communication: To send you push notifications about relevant interactions (likes, follows, whispers) if you grant permission.
4. Third-Party Sharing and Selling of Data
We do not sell your personal data to third parties for money. However, under certain laws like the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), sharing data for targeted advertising may be considered a "sale" or "share." We share your data with the following categories of third parties:

Service Providers: Cloud storage and database providers (e.g., Supabase, Cloudflare R2, Firebase) used strictly to host your UGC and account data.
Advertising Partners: Google AdMob, which collects device identifiers and usage data to serve advertisements.
Analytics Providers: Crashlytics or similar tools to monitor app stability.
5. Data Retention Practices
We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy:

Account Data: Retained as long as your account is active.
User Generated Content: Retained until you delete the video/comment or delete your account.
Logs & Technical Data: Typically deleted or anonymized within 90 days. When data is no longer needed, it is securely deleted or anonymized.
6. Your Rights Under GDPR (For EU/UK Users)
If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA) or the UK, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

Right of Access: You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
Right to Rectification (Correction): You have the right to request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
Right to Erasure (Deletion): You have the right to request that we delete your personal data (the "right to be forgotten").
Right to Restrict/Object to Processing: You can object to how we use your data, especially for direct marketing or advertising.
Right to Data Portability: You can request your data in a structured, commonly used format.
To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at the email address provided in Section 1.

7. Your Rights Under CCPA and US State Laws (For US Users)
If you are a resident of California or other applicable US states, you have specific rights regarding your personal information:

Right to Know: You have the right to request information about the categories and specific pieces of personal data we have collected, the sources, the business purpose, and the categories of third parties with whom it is shared.
Right to Delete: You have the right to request the deletion of your personal data.
Right to Correct: You have the right to request correction of inaccurate data.
Right to Know About Sales/Sharing: You have the right to know whether your personal data is sold or shared and to whom.
Right to Opt-Out of Sale or Sharing: You have the right to direct us not to sell or share your personal information. You can exercise this right by adjusting your ad preferences in the app settings or opting out of personalized ads via your device settings.
Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights.
To exercise your US state privacy rights, please contact us at the email address provided in Section 1.
